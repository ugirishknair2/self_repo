In computing, plain text is a loose term for data (e.g. file contents) that represent only 
characters of readable material but not its graphical representation nor other objects (floating-point numbers, images, etc.). 
It may also include a limited number of "whitespace" characters that affect simple arrangement of text, 
such as spaces, line breaks, or tabulation characters. Plain text is different from formatted text, 
where style information is included; from structured text, where structural parts of the document such as paragraphs, 
sections, and the like are identified; and from binary files
